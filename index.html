About me

I am a data scientist and recent graduate of Georgetown University's M.S. in Data Science and Analytics (DSAN) Program. At Georgetown, I studied many data-related topics, such as machine learning, neural networks, statistics, data visualization, programming, and database design.

Before returning to graduate school to focus on data science full-time, I was a research analyst in Penn State's Cognitive Neuroscience of Creativity Lab (CNCL). My primary work at the CNCL focused on developing machine learning techniques for the automated assessment of creative ability and conducting statistical analyses on the results of our experiments. I also managed neuroimaging studies that incorporated tools including functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and functional near infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) to examine what creativity "looks like" in the brain.

I graduated with my bachelor's degree in psychology from the University of Virginia, where I completed my undergraduate thesis on the complexity of electroencephalogram (EEG) signals during the experience of pleasant and unpleasant emotions. During college, I interned with the United States House of Representatives, where I researched mental health legislation.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, hiking, snowboarding, going to concerts, and traveling.
